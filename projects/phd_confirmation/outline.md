## Scenario 1: What are Bound Harmonics? (wave group)

**Goal**: Give the committee an intuitive picture of bound harmonics
before introducing the theory. Show both spatial and spectral signatures.

### 起 — Setup
- A clean linear wave group: Gaussian envelope × carrier wave
- Show it in spatial domain; show its narrow-band spectrum at k₀

### 承 — Build
- Add 2nd-order super-harmonic (bound, ~ε·a·cos 2k₀x)
  → new spectral peak appears at 2k₀
- Add 2nd-order set-down (bound long wave, negative depression under group)
  → spectral energy appears near k≈0

### 转 — Core insight
- Reveal the total nonlinear wave: crests sharpen, troughs flatten
- Animate time evolution: the entire nonlinear structure (harmonics + set-down)
  travels together with the group at c_g — they are *locked* to it

### 合 — Takeaway
- Text annotation: "Bound harmonics ≠ free waves; they do not satisfy the
  dispersion relation independently — they are slaved to the primary wave group"
